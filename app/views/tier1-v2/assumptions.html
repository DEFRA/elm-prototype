Prototype assumptions

The prototype is built on the following assumptions:

  1. Users can self-serve (no advisor required).
  2. A single user, with a single holding, can hold a single agreement.
  3. The service will support straight through processing.
  4. Tier 1 options (including standards and packages) are not at the parcel level.
  5. Evidence for eligibility will be asked for by exception.
  6. Payments could be made on a monthly basis.

Working definitions

  • Application - The creation and submission of an ELM plan
  • Plan (land management plan) - One or more options that a land manager (farmer/forester/agent/advisor etc) wants to do on their land, with a clear understanding of how and when to do it, and what it’s worth
  • Agreement - Created after a successful application is made, including plan details and payment schedule which must be accepted by the applicant to be paid and fulfilled
  • Option - An objective that consists of an outcome and/or one or more actions that is chosen by a land manager to include in an ELM plan
  • Package - A group of two or more actions or options chosen by the user to include in an ELM plan. (Grouped by e.g. land type, farm type, region)
  • Standard - One or more mandatory actions which meet an environmental assurance level which a land manager can choose to include in an ELM plan. (Standards created by e.g. National, land type, farm type, region, outcome, action, prohibitions, strategy)

Overarching hypotheses

  • RELEVANCY - We believe that if we provide users with relevant information at the right time they will have greater confidence to make informed decisions.
  • SIMPLICITY - We believe that if we create a simple application process, aligned to the farmer/forester’s mental model, more users will be able to self-serve.
  • FLEXIBILITY - We believe that a more flexible service which fits with a user’s farm and situation will encourage uptake and fulfilment of agreements.
