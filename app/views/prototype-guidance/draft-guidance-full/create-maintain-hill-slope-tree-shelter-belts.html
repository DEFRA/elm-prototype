Guidance

Create or maintain hill slope tree shelter belts to reduce flooding

Find out how to create or maintain a shelter belt of trees on hill slopes to protect land from flooding.

Contents

  1. Benefits of hillslope tree shelter belts
  2. Where you can create hillslope tree shelter belts
  3. How to create and maintain a hillslope tree shelter belt

Benefits of hillslope tree shelter belts

Tree shelter belts on hillslopes will:

  • reduce the risk of flood
  • improve water quality
  • prevent soil erosion
  • support wildlife
  • enhance the landscape

Where you can create hillslope tree shelter belts

You can create a shelter belt of trees on:

  • bare land (any land that has not been woodland for at least 10 years)
  • woodland where trees have been felled due to a disease
  • hedgerows

You should not create tree shelter belts:

  • if tree planting could damage areas of ecological, archaeological or historic interest
  • to plant woodland for producing Christmas trees or biomass
  • for restocking woodlands following a conditional felling licence (except where this has been issued for tree health reasons)
  • to plant ash trees

How to create and maintain a hillslope tree shelter belt

Use the FC tree species selection tool to find the right species for your soil and climate conditions.

Create a suitable planting position by spraying herbicide in advance. Use a scarifier to create lines or prepare a bare patch of land (screefing) with a spade. Remove weeds by hand, or by using a suitable mulch or herbicide.

Choose the right planting method

Use a planting method that helps survival, with roots fully inserted in the ground with the root collar at ground level - the tree must be firmed in without damaging the stem.

Dead plants can be replaced in the following planting season.

Protect your tree shelter

Protect young trees by attaching a spiral secured with a cane. This will stop animals from damaging the trees. Keep them until the trees are big enough to no longer need protection. You can use tree shelters instead of spiral guards.
